Birayni

Description

Biryani is a rich and aromatic rice dish made with basmati rice, marinated meat (chicken, beef, or mutton), and a blend of fragrant spices. It is a beloved dish in South Asian cuisine, known for its layered cooking method, where the rice and meat are slow-cooked together, absorbing all the rich flavors. Biryani is often garnished with fried onions, boiled eggs, and fresh coriander, making it a dish fit for celebrations and special occasions.

Ingredient

    For the Meat Marinade:

    • 1 kg chicken, beef, or mutton (cut into medium pieces)
    • 1 cup yogurt
    • 2 tbsp ginger paste
    • 2 tbsp garlic paste
    • 2 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 2 tsp coriander powder
    • 2 tsp cumin powder
    • 1 tsp garam masala powder
    • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
    • ½ cup fried onions (beresta)
    • 2 tbsp lemon juice
    • 3 tbsp mustard oil or ghee

    For the Rice:

    • 3 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
    • 6 cups water
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 3-4 cloves
    • 3-4 cardamoms
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • 1 star anise (optional)
    • 1 tsp salt

    For Assembling:

    • ½ cup fried onions (beresta)
    • 2 tbsp saffron-infused milk (or food color mixed with milk)
    • 2 tbsp melted ghee
    • 2 boiled eggs (for garnish)
    • Fresh coriander & mint leaves

Steps to Cook Biryani

    Step 1: Marinate the Meat

    1. In a large bowl, mix the meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, coriander, cumin, garam masala, salt, fried onions, lemon juice, and mustard oil/ghee.
    2. Let it marinate for at least 2-4 hours (overnight for best flavor).

    Step 2: Prepare the Rice

    1. In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add bay leaves, cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon, star anise, and salt.
    2. Add the soaked basmati rice and cook until it is 70% cooked (it should still be firm). Drain and set aside.

    Step 3: Cook the Meat

    1. Heat oil or ghee in a deep pot and add the marinated meat.
    2. Cook on medium heat for about 30-40 minutes (for beef/mutton) or 15-20 minutes (for chicken) until tender.
    3. If needed, add a little water to prevent burning but keep the gravy thick.

    Step 4: Layering the Biryani

    1. In the same pot with the cooked meat, start layering

    Step 5: Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking)

    1. Cover the pot tightly with a lid or seal with dough to trap the steam.
    2. Cook on low heat for 20-30 minutes to let the flavors infuse.

    Step 6: Serve & Enjoy

    1. Gently mix the layers before serving.
    2. Garnish with boiled eggs, fried onions, and fresh herbs.
    3. Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or salad.
